# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Financial services firms — banks, investment companies, KYC providers — who need to onboard clients through structured forms. Admins at these firms manage form templates, review submissions, and track onboarding status. End clients fill out KYC questionnaires, loan applications, and investment declarations. The primary audience is the administrators; end clients are secondary users who interact with the form-rendering surface.

## Product Purpose

Replace fragmented onboarding workflows (email attachments, paper forms, generic form builders) with a unified platform purpose-built for financial services compliance. Admins create dynamic forms with conditional logic; clients submit structured data; staff review and approve submissions. Success means faster onboarding cycles, fewer errors, and audit-ready records.

## Positioning

Dynamic forms that adapt to financial services needs without enterprise bloat.

## Brand Personality

Professional, trustworthy, clear. The voice is confident and direct — no jargon, no filler. Tone adapts: reassuring during errors, efficient during tasks, quiet during success. The interface should feel like a competent human assistant: calm authority without coldness.

## Design Principles

1. **Flexible without complexity** — Every feature should feel simple even when it's powerful. Conditional logic, dynamic fields, and multi-step workflows should be discoverable, not hidden.
2. **Clarity over decoration** — White space, typography hierarchy, and purposeful color. No visual noise that doesn't help the user complete their task.
3. **Trust through transparency** — Status visibility, clear error messages, and honest copy. Users should always know where they stand.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Show what's needed now, reveal complexity only when the user needs it. The dashboard should feel calm, not overwhelming.
5. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA compliance, keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Accessibility is a feature, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Keyboard navigation throughout. Screen reader support with proper ARIA labels. Focus-visible states on all interactive elements. Reduced motion support via prefers-reduced-motion. Sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 minimum for body text).
